Customer Experience

How I approach CX.

Most CX problems aren’t really about the people on the front line. They’re about how the work is set up — the systems, the processes, the handoffs, the tools, the way information flows (or doesn’t). Fix those and the customer experience follows.

Diagnosis & design
  • Customer journey mapping and service blueprinting
  • Root-cause analysis of recurring CX failures
  • Quality assurance framework design
  • Complaint analysis and systemic improvement
  • Customer feedback loop design and closed-loop resolution
Operations & strategy
  • Contact centre operations and process redesign
  • Voice of Customer (VoC) program design
  • CX metrics strategy — NPS, CSAT, CES, and what actually matters
  • Training, coaching, and knowledge management alignment
  • Compliance integration without compromising experience

I’ve developed and refined this approach across contact centres, government-contracted programmes, and compliance-heavy environments in New Zealand and Australia. That includes work delivered on behalf of the Australian Commonwealth Department of Health and Aged Care and New South Wales State Government electoral services. I know what works when compliance isn’t optional, and I know how to balance operational efficiency with customer experience — because I’ve had to do both.

Whether it’s a full CX strategy or a focused improvement in one area, I work directly with your team to make it happen — no lengthy change programmes, no unnecessary complexity.

If any of this sounds familiar, I can probably help.

  • Your NPS, CSAT, or customer satisfaction metrics have been flat for months and nobody can explain why
  • Your contact centre has inconsistent quality, high turnover, or no clear framework for how work should be done
  • You’re getting the same complaints over and over, and they never seem to get properly fixed
  • You’re launching a new service or product and need the customer experience right from day one
  • You have customer feedback coming in but no structured way to act on it or track whether things improve
  • Compliance requirements are creating friction for customers, and you want to find a smarter way to meet them

About

I’ve spent the better part of a decade working across customer experience, operations, and compliance. Contact centres, retail, healthcare support, hospitality, in New Zealand and Australia. Different industries, but a lot of the same problems.

That work has taken me into some complex environments: operations delivered on behalf of the Australian Commonwealth Department of Health and Aged Care, New South Wales State Government electoral services, and large-scale contact centres where compliance, quality, and consistency aren’t optional. I hold an RG146 Tier 1 Superannuation certification and a BA in Political Science from the University of Waikato.

Over that time I kept coming back to the same thought. So much of the friction in business came down to tools and systems that just weren’t built for how people actually work. So I started building my own. What began as a way to solve specific problems became a genuine passion for development, and eventually Parallax Labs.

These days I run Parallax Labs, an AI software studio based in Auckland. For me the consulting and the development are the same thing. I like working directly with businesses on the stuff that’s frustrating them, not just building software about it.

If something isn’t working the way it should, I’m usually pretty good at figuring out why.
